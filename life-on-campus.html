College Life In America

As an international student, perhaps much of your knowledge stems from what you've gleaned from pop culture tv shows and movies that depict a certian kind of American Lifestyle. While pop culture can be a great referenc point for various American quirks, often times it's much more exaggerated than reality. College life at Washington University is hard work- but life in a dorm room shouldn't have to be! So before you can sit back and get comfey on your tempurpedic matress, take a look at some of the housing options you'll have next year, and some common questions you and your fellow international students might have!

Will I be paired with other international students?

In order to get you well integrated into the Wash U campus, you will be paired with other American freshman or sophomore students depending on your year in school.

What if I have problems with my roommate(s)?

Your RA's, or residential advisors that live on every floor of every building, are specially trained to help out with problems and disputes between roommates. Don't feel like you have to bear all the weight of your problem- speak up and ask for help!

What if those problems are race/culture related?

Again, talking to your RA is your best option here since every situation is different and they are specially trained to deal with these kinds of things. Keep in mind that most students on campus are quite open minded- it's something we as a univeristy pride ourselves on- so realize any problem your room mate has with you is between you and them and has no reflection on the opinions of the campus body as a whole.
